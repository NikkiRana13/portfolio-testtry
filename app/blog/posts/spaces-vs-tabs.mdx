# 🎓 A Realistic Guide to Navigating Senior Year

Senior year feels like a lot because… it is. One second you’re stressing about a chem test, and the next you’re supposed to make *life decisions* that apparently define your future. Everyone suddenly has opinions, deadlines sneak up on you, and it feels like there’s never enough time.

This isn’t some perfect “how to” guide. I’m just someone who’s gone through this already — and learned a lot the hard way. I hope this helps make your year a little less overwhelming, and maybe even a bit exciting.

---

## 1. Figuring Out What You Actually Want

Let’s start with the scariest part: **what comes next**.

When people ask “What are you doing after high school?” it can feel like everyone already has a plan but you. (They don’t, by the way. Most people are winging it.)

Take a second to slow down and actually think about *you*:

* What subjects or activities make time disappear when you’re doing them?
* What kind of work environment do you actually like — structured? creative? flexible?
* What kind of life do you want long-term, not just what sounds impressive?

Do your research, but look beyond course calendars. Look up what grads are actually doing a few years out. Where are they working? What are their salaries like? Are they excited about their field? This part isn’t about getting everything right — it’s about learning enough to make a **good first step**.

And yes, it’s okay to factor in money, stability, and opportunity. That doesn’t make you boring or greedy — it makes you smart.

## 2. Building Your Foundation Early

Here’s a secret no one really tells you: future-you will thank past-you if you just **write stuff down now**.

Start a simple **master document** with everything you do outside (and inside) of school. It doesn’t have to look fancy. Here’s a quick example of what it can look like:

| Activity               | Dates       | Description                                        | Impact/Highlights                               | Contact/Ref |
|-------------------------|------------|----------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| Student Council         | 2022–2024  | Planned assemblies, spirit weeks, and ran announcements. | Co-led winter fundraiser, $800 raised.         | Ms. Brown   |
| Volunteer Tutor         | Fall 2023  | Tutored Grade 10 math students after school.       | Helped 6 students raise grades by at least 1 level | Mr. Lee   |
| CTPC Coding Tournament  | Spring 2024| Helped run national-level student-led event.       | 800+ attendees, $7K sponsorship raised.         | Myself lol  |

This one document will save your life when it’s time for:

* Scholarship essays
* Resume writing
* Supplemental applications
* Reference letters

Also make:

* A few **resume templates** (e.g. one for school, one for jobs, one for extracurricular stuff)
* A list of programs with requirements, deadlines, and a few personal notes (why you like or don’t like them)
* A simple scholarship tracker — literally a spreadsheet works

**September is chaos.** If you prep before then, you’re already ahead of most people.

---

## 3. Balancing School and Life

Here’s the thing: grades matter, but they’re not your entire personality.

Senior year gets intense. It’s easy to fall into the trap of living in survival mode — studying until 2AM every night, saying no to everything fun, and feeling like if you take one break you’ll fall behind forever. Don’t do that to yourself.

Some things that help:

* Treat school like a 9–5. Once the “work day” is done, give yourself actual rest.
* Schedule in breaks and social things — seriously, put them in your calendar.
* Pick extracurriculars you *like*. Not just resume boosters.
* Be honest with yourself about burnout. If you need to say no, say no.

You’re not a robot. Balance doesn’t make you lazy. It keeps you from falling apart.

## 4. Application Season Strategy

Applications feel like they’re designed to stress you out — but if you break them into small steps, they’re a lot less terrifying.

For each school:

* Apply early through Ontario Universities' Application Centre (OUAC) or your province’s portal.
* Get your **student ID** early to access forms and portals.
* Don’t wait until the last second to do bursaries and scholarships.
* If something’s optional, **do it anyway** — optional is usually code for “this gives you an edge.”

When it comes to essays and short answers, use the **STAR Method**:

* **Situation:** set the scene
* **Task:** your role
* **Action:** what you did
* **Result:** what happened because of it

And please — stop underselling yourself. Even small things count.

---

## 5. Dealing with Stress and Exam Season

Waiting for responses is brutal. It feels like your whole future is in a mystery box.

During that time, the best thing you can do is **take care of the things you can control**:

* Make a simple, realistic study plan.
* Use practice tests and old notes — don’t just reread stuff.
* Actually sleep.
* Eat something other than instant ramen at 2AM.
* Go outside. Movement matters more than people admit.

Even if you’ve already been accepted somewhere, exams still matter — they can affect scholarships, placement, or just how good you feel walking out of high school. Don’t let yourself coast too hard at the end.

---

## 6. Financial Prep: Understanding the Numbers

Money is a huge part of post-secondary that nobody really prepares you for. It’s not fun to talk about, but it’s better to face it head-on now than panic later.

Start by making a basic budget:

* Tuition & fees
* Residence or rent
* Meal plans or groceries
* Transportation
* Toiletries, phone, personal stuff
* Fun money (yes, this matters)
* Emergency cushion if possible

Look into OSAP or whatever aid your province has. Apply for scholarships even if they seem small — they *add up*. Talk openly with your family (if you can) about what they can help with so you’re not surprised in August.

Also: learn how to track your spending. Even a basic note app works.

---

## 7. Acceptance — and What Comes Next

The moment that email hits your inbox, it’s a mix of shock, relief, and panic. Suddenly the thing you’ve spent months worrying about is real.

But here’s something people don’t say enough: **this summer is special**. It’s the last real stretch before everything shifts.
Use it. Spend time with people who matter. Do dumb little things you love. Let yourself breathe.

University will be intense and messy and wonderful. You’ll figure it out.

---

## 8. Final Note: You’ve Got This

No one has it fully figured out. Everyone is pretending to be more put together than they are. You don’t need to be perfect — just persistent.

Senior year is a lot. But you’re going to get through it. Step by step, form by form, late-night cry by late-night cry. And somewhere in the middle of it all, you’ll start building the life you actually want.

So take a deep breath. You’ve got this.
